Vegetable Chili

Adapted from the Engine 2 Cookbook Magazine. A vegetable chili made with real ingredients. You won't believe there's no meat! Spices can be adjusted to your taste.

Ingredients

  • 1.5 cup(250 g) dry pinto(うずら豆) beans
  • 4 cups cooked brown rice
  • 1.5 onion, diced
  • 1 bell pepper, chopped
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 Tbsp oregano
  • 3 Tbsp chili powder
  • 1 Tbsp cumin
  • 2 15oz cans of diced tomatoes
  • salt and black pepper to taste
  • Optional: ~3 Bay leaves for extra flavor

Steps

  1. Start with 2 15 oz cans of pinto beans or put 250 g of dry pinto beans in a large pot and fill with water about 2.5 cm (1 inch) above beans and boil for 3 min and let sit covered for 1 hr.
  2. Cook about 4 cups of cooked rice. Start with 2 cups (360 g) of dry rice and cook in rice pot or on stove.
  3. Cut onions and bell pepper into 1 cm sized pieces and put into pot.
  4. Mince garlic and keep on the side.
  5. Cook the onions and bell pepper until soft, on medium heat adding water to prevent sticking.
  6. Once onions are clear and soft, add garlic, oregano, chili powder, and cumin.
  7. Stir vigorously for 1 minute to release flavor from spices.
  8. Add beans with liquid(or rinse beans and use water/vegetable stock to reduce stomach gas) to pot.
  9. Mix well and add diced tomatoes to the pot.
  10. Bring to a boil and reduce heat to low.
  11. Simmer and let liquid reduce for 20min or until tomatoes start to break down and chili becomes thick.
  12. Add salt and black pepper to taste.
